I quote fixed prices. Every engagement, agreed in writing before any work starts. Here’s the thinking behind it.

## Hourly billing rewards the wrong things

When you bill by the hour, the slow, meandering version of the work pays better than the fast, focused one. That’s a strange incentive to put between a studio and the people it’s trying to help. It also means you can’t really budget — the final number is a mystery until the invoice lands.

## Fixed scope keeps both sides honest

Before anything starts, we write down exactly what’s included and what isn’t. You know the price. I know the boundaries. Nobody’s watching a meter.

- **You can budget.** The number is the number.
- **I’m rewarded for being good, not slow.** Working efficiently helps both of us.
- **Scope creep is a conversation, not a surprise.** New requests get re-quoted openly, never quietly billed.

## The one exception

Ongoing support is the exception, because “keep helping as things come up” genuinely doesn’t have a fixed scope. That’s what the retainer is for — a monthly block of hours at a clear rate.

## Start small if you want

If committing to a fixed price still feels like a leap with someone new, the $500 Infrastructure Audit is a low-risk way to see how I work before a bigger build.

Clear scope, clear price, written down. It’s better for everyone — including me.
